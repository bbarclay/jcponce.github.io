Roots of a complex number

\(n\) roots of \(z=e^{i\theta}\), \(n>1\) means: We want all \(w\in \C\) such that \(w^n=z\).

Notation: \(\exp(\xi)= e^{\xi}\).

Roots of a complex number

\(z\) has \(n\) distinct \(n\)th roots:

\[{\small \begin{align*} & \left\{ z^{1/n}\exp\left(\frac{i\theta}{n}\right), z^{1/n}\exp\left(\frac{i\theta}{n} + \frac{i2\pi}{n}\right), \right. \\ & \quad \left. z^{1/n}\exp\left(\frac{i\theta}{n} + \frac{i4\pi}{n}\right), \ldots, z^{1/n}\exp\left(\frac{i\theta}{n} + \frac{i2(n-1)\pi}{n} \right) \right\} \end{align*}} \]

Functions and mappings

\(\Omega \subseteq \C\): A function \(f:\Omega \rightarrow \C\) can be viewed as a mapping on \(\Omega\), the domain of \(f\).

If \(\Omega \) is not speficified, take \(\Omega \) as large as possible.

For example for \(f(z)=\frac{1}{z}\), the domain is \(\C_*\) and \(f:\C_*\rightarrow \C\).

We can write: \(f:z \mapsto \frac{1}{z}\); or \(w=\frac{1}{z}\); or just \(1/z\).

Functions and mappings

Usual notation: \(w: (x,y) \mapsto (u,v)\) i. e., \[ \begin{align*} w(x+iy) &= u(x+iy)+iv(x+iy)\\ & \text{ or} & \\ w(x, y) &= u(x,y)+iv(x, y) \end{align*} \]

Functions and mappings

\(*\, \Omega = \) domain of \( f = \) dom\(\left(\,f\right)\).

\(*\,\) Range\(\left(\,f\right)\): \[ f\left(\Omega\right) = \left\{ w: w=f(z)\text{ for some }z\in\C\right\}. \]

\(*\, f^{-1}\left(\xi \right) = \left\{ z\in\C: f(z)=\xi \right\}\) inverse of \(f\)
(not \(1/f\)). NOT necessarily a function!

dom\(\left(\,f\right) = \C_*\),

\( \;f^{-1}(\xi)=1/\xi\) is a function.

Functions and mappings

Example 2   -   \(f(z)=\dfrac{1}{1-|z|^2}\).

dom\(\left(g\right) = \left\{ z:|z|\neq 1\right\}\),

\(g: \left\{ z:|z|\neq 1\right\}\rightarrow \R\).

Inverse is not a function.

Check: If \(g(z)=\rho\), then \(g(e^{i\theta}z)=\rho\) for any \(\theta \in \R\).
