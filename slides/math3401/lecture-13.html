Limits

Let \(f\) be a \(\C\)-valued function defined on a deleted neighbourhood of \(z\in\C\).

\(\displaystyle \lim_{z\ra z_0}f(z)=w_0\) says: Given \(\epsilon > 0\) exists \(\delta > 0\) such that \[0<|z-z_0|<\delta \implies \left|f(z)-w_0\right|<\epsilon.\]

Note: \(f\) does not have to be defined at \(z_0\).

Limit Theorems

Suppose \(f(z) = f(x+iy) = u(x,y) + i v(x,y) \), \[z_0= x_0+iy_0 \text{ and } w_0 =u_0+iv_0.\]

Theorem 1: \[ \lim_{z \ra z_0} f(z)=w_0 \iff \left\{ \begin{array}{rl} \displaystyle\lim_{(x,y)\ra (x_0+iy_0)} u(x,y) & = u_0,\\ \displaystyle\lim_{(x,y)\ra (x_0+iy_0)} v(x,y) & = v_0. \end{array} \right. \]

Limit Theorems

Theorem 2: Suppose \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}f(z) = w_0\) and \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}g(z) = w_0\) and \(\lambda\in \C\). Then

  1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}\left(f \pm g\right)(z) = w_0 \pm \xi_0\);
  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}\left(\lambda \cdot f \right)(z) = \lambda \cdot w_0\);
  3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}\left(f \cdot g\right)(z) = w_0 \cdot \xi_0\);
  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{z \ra z_0}\dfrac{f(z)}{g(z)} = \dfrac{w_0}{\xi_0}\), as long as \(\xi_0\neq 0\).
