2.3 Contour diagrams

Geographical maps have curves of constant height above sea level, or curves of constant air pressure (isobars), or curves of constant temperature (isothermals). Drawing contours is an effective method of representing a 3-dimensional surface in two dimensions. We now look at functions $f$ of two variables. A contour is a curve corresponding to the equation $z=f(x,y)=C,$ see also Stewart, Section 14.1.
