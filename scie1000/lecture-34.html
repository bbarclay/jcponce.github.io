Predator-prey systems


12.3 Eat or be eaten

  • In addition to modelling individual organisms with multiple life stages, systems of DEs can also model interactions between multiple species.
  • For example, the classical predator/prey problem in ecology considers what happens to the populations of two species when one preys on the other.
  • In laboratory situations there is control over these interactions. In nature, interspecies interactions are highly complex. We will first investigate a controlled example, then model a real interaction. The controlled example is very simple, but is not completely unreasonable.

Case Study 29: It's just not cricket

  • Now we will develop a more realistic predator/prey model. In general, such models are based on the assumptions that:
    • the prey has no other predators, and the predator no other prey;
    • there is no significant change to the environment or species' genetics;
    • the prey species is not resource limited and so grows rapidly; prey growth is regulated solely by predator consumption.
    • predator population growth depends on the availability of prey; the predator declines to extinction in the absence of prey.
    • there is no limit on the amount of prey the predators are able to consume.



Case Study 29: It's just not cricket

  • The best-known predator/prey model is the Lotka-Volterra model.

Lotka-Volterra model

Let $P(t)$ and $Q(t)$ be the population sizes of a predator and prey species respectively, at any time $t$. The following system of DEs forms the Lotka-Volterra model:

$ \begin{eqnarray*} Q' = & \,\;aQ - b PQ \\ P' = & -cP + d PQ \\ \end{eqnarray*} $

where $a,$ $b,$ $c$ and $d$ are positive constants whose values depend on various characteristics of the species and their physical interactions.

Program specifications: Develop a Python program that uses Euler's method with a step size of 0.01 years to model the populations of lynx and hares.

Program 12.2: Lotka-Volterra model of hares and lynx


Program 12.2: Lotka-Volterra model of hares and lynx

# Uses Euler's method and Lotka-Volterra equations to 
# model populations of lynx and hare from 1900 to 1920.
from pylab import *

# Initialise variables for Euler's method.
stepsize = 0.01
times = arange(0, 20 + stepsize, stepsize )
a = 0.484
b = 0.028
c = 1
d = 0.032
hares = zeros(int(size(times))) 
lynx = zeros(int(size(times))) 
hares[0] = 30.0
lynx[0] = 4.0


Program 12.2: Lotka-Volterra model of hares and lynx

# Step through Euler' s method
for i in range(0,size(times)-1):
    dh = a * hares[i] - b * hares[i] * lynx[i]
    dl = - c * lynx[i] + d * hares[i] * lynx[i] 
    hares[i+1] = hares[i] + stepsize * dh 
    lynx[i+1] = lynx[i] + stepsize * dl

# Output graphs 
plot(times, hares, 'b-', linewidth=2, label='Hares') 
plot(times , lynx, 'r-', linewidth=2, label='Lynx') 
xlabel("Time ( years since 1900)")
ylabel("Number of individuals (thousands)")
xlim(0 ,20)
ylim(0 ,70)
grid(True)
legend()
show()
