Classification of Singularities

For an isolated singularity \(z_0\):

  1. Removable.
  2. Pole.
  3. Essential.

Classification of Singularities

If \(z_0 = 0\), then

  1. \(\ds \sum_{n\ge 0}a_n z^n\)
  2. \(\ds \sum_{n\ge - N} a_n z^n\)   \(N\gt 1, a_N\neq 0\)
  3. \(\ds \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n z^n \)   (no \(m\) such that \(a_m=0\) for all \(n\lt m\))

Classification of Singularities

Example 1:  \(f(z) = \dfrac{\sin z}{z}\), singularity at \(z=0\).

  • \(f\) has a removable singularity at \(z=0\).
  • \(f(z) = \ds\frac{1}{z}\left(z- \frac{z^3}{3!} + \cdots\right)\) \(=1- \dfrac{z^2}{3!}+ \cdots\).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = 0\).

Classification of Singularities

Example 2:  \(f(z) = \dfrac{z^2-1}{z^2-1}\).

  • \(f\) has two removable singularity at \(z=\pm 1\).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = 0\).

Classification of Singularities

Example 3:  \(f(z) = \dfrac{1}{z^4}\), isolated singularity at \(z=0\).

  • \(f\) has a pole of order \(4\).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = 0\).

Classification of Singularities

Example 3':  \(f(z) = \dfrac{1}{z^4} + \dfrac{1}{z^2}\), isolated singularity at \(z=0\).

  • \(f\) has a pole of order \(4\).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = 0\).

Classification of Singularities

Example 4:  \(f(z) = \dfrac{\sinh z^2}{z^7} \), isolated singularity at \(z=0\).

  • \(f\) has a pole of order \(5\).
  • \(f(z) = \ds \frac{1}{z^5} + \frac{1}{z}\frac{1}{3!}+\cdots \).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = \dfrac{1}{3}\).

Classification of Singularities

Example 5:  \(f(z) = e^{1/z} \), isolated singularity at \(z=0\).

  • \(f\) has an essential singularity at \(z=0\).
  • \(f(z) = \ds 1+\frac{1}{z} +\frac{1}{z^2} \frac{1}{2!} + \cdots \).
  • \(\underset{z=0}{\res} f(z) = 1\).

Picard's Theorem

Theorem: If \(f\) has an essential singularity at \(z_0\) then on \(B_R\setminus\{z_0\}\) \(f\) attains every value (in \(\C\)) infinitely often, with the possible exception of one point.
