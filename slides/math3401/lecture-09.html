Branches

Branch: interval of a function \( \alpha \leq \hat{\theta} < \alpha + 2 \pi \), or \(\alpha < \hat{\theta} \leq \alpha + 2 \pi\), for some \(\alpha \in \R.\)

We can define a single-valued \(\arg\) with values in that interval and hence a single valued \(\log\), and a single-valued branch of, e. g., \(z^{1/2}\).

Branch cut

\(\left\{ z: \arg(z) = \alpha \right\} \cup \left\{ 0 \right\}\) subset of \(\C\).

E. g. PV\(\left(z^{1/2}\right)\): \[ \begin{align*} & z\mapsto |z|^{1/2}\exp\left(\frac{i\Arg (z)}{2}\right) \\ &re^{i \theta} \mapsto \sqrt{r}\exp \left(\frac{i\theta}{2}\right), -\pi < \theta \leq \pi. \end{align*} \]

Branch is \(-\pi < \theta \leq \pi\), and the branch cut is the positive real axis union with \(\left\{ 0 \right\}\).

Trigonometric functions

For \(x\in \R\) \[ \begin{align*} e^{ix} &= \cos x + i \sin x \quad (1)\\ e^{-ix} &= \cos x - i \sin x \quad (2) \end{align*} \]

\((1) + (2)\) implies \[ \cos x = \frac{e^{ix}+ e^{-ix}}{2} \quad (3)' \]

\((1) - (2)\) implies \[ \sin x = \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} \quad (4)' \]

Trigonometric functions

Use (3)' and (4)' to define \(\cos\) and \(\sin\) on \(\C\), i. e.: \[ \begin{align*} \cos z &= \frac{e^{iz}+ e^{-iz}}{2}\\ \sin z &= \frac{e^{iz}+ e^{-iz}}{2i}. \end{align*} \]

Trigonometric functions

There hold: \[ \begin{align*} \cos z &= \cos(-z) \quad (3)\\ \sin z &= -\sin(-z) \quad (4) \end{align*} \]

\[ \begin{align*} \cos (z + \xi) &= \cos z \cos \xi - \sin z \sin \xi \quad (5)\\ \sin (z + \xi) &= \sin z \cos \xi + \cos z \sin \xi \quad (6) \end{align*} \]

\[ \begin{align*} \sin^2 z + \cos^2 z &= 1 \quad (7) \end{align*} \]

\[ \begin{align*} \sin \left(z+\frac{\pi}{2}\right) &= \cos z \quad (8)\\ \sin \left(z-\frac{\pi}{2}\right) &= -\cos z \quad (9) \end{align*} \]

Hyperbolic functions

Recall hyperbolic functions on \(\R\) \[ \begin{align*} \text{PV}\left(z^{c}\right) &= \exp \big(c\,\Log (z)\big) \\ &= \exp\big(c \ln |z| + i \Arg (z)\big). \end{align*} \]

E. g. \(\text{PV}\left[(1+i)^{4i}\right]\):

\(\qquad\qquad =\exp\big( 4i \left[ \ln |1-i| + i \Arg (1-i) \right] \big)\)

\(\qquad\qquad =\exp\big( 4i\ln\sqrt{2} -4(\pi/4) \big)\)

\(\qquad\qquad =e^{\pi} \exp \left(4i\ln \sqrt{2}\right)\)

\(\qquad\qquad =e^{\pi} \cos \left(2\ln 2\right) + i e^{\pi} \sin \left(2\ln 2\right)\).

Remark

Sometimes need to use a different single-valued \(\log \) and \(\arg\), e. g. may nee to integrate around a contour.

In this case, choose/define \(\arg(z)\) such that \[ \frac{\pi}{2}< \arg (z) \leq \frac{3\pi}{2} \] where this \(\arg(z)\) is a particlar value of \(\arg(z)\), which leads to a new, single-valued \(\Log\), etc.
