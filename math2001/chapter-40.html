40.1 Parametric surfaces

Another way of representing a surface $S$ in $\R^3$ is by a parametrisation. This is where the coordinate variables are functions of two parameters $u$ and $v$: $$ x=x(u,v),\ \ y=y(u,v),\ \ z=z(u,v) $$ and the vector $$ \r(u,v) = x(u,v)\i+y(u,v)\j+z(u,v)\k $$ traces out the surface as $u,v$ vary over some region $D$ in the "$uv$-plane". So for every point $(u,v)$ in $D$, there corresponds a point on the surface $S$.
