Physical problems

Physical configurations often modeled by solutions of partial differential equations.

Generally, interested in solving a PDE subject to associated initial/boundary conditions.

Dirichlet problem

\( \text{(D) } \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} \Delta u = 0 & \text{in } \Omega\\ u|_{\partial \Omega} = \varphi & (*) \end{array} \right. \)

\((*)\) Says \(u(\mathbf x)= \varphi(\mathbf x)\) for all \(\mathbf x \in \partial \Omega\)   (\(\varphi: \partial \Omega \ra \R\))

\(\Omega, \varphi\) are known/given. \(u\) is the unknown.

\(\text{(D)}\) is called Dirichlet problem for Laplace's equation, a. k. a. boundary problem of the first find.

Dirichlet problem

One way to "solve" \(\text{(D)}\) is to find \(u\) that minimises \[\int_{\Omega} \left|\nabla u\right|^2 d\mathbf x\] subject to \(u|_{\partial \Omega} = \varphi\).

Neumann problem

Also important: Boundary conditions of the second kind, called Neumann boundary conditions

\( \text{(N) } \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} \Delta u = 0 & \\ \displaystyle\frac{\partial u}{\partial \mathbf v} = \psi & \text{on } \partial \Omega \end{array} \right. \)

In practice often have homogeneous Neumann boundary conditions, i. e. \(\psi = 0\).
