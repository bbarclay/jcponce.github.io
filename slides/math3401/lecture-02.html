Introduction

  • \(\mathbb N\): \(\{1, 2, 3, \ldots \}\) natural numbers
  • \(\mathbb N_0\): \(\{0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots \}\)
  • \(\mathbb Z\): \(\{0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots \}\) integers
  • \(\mathbb Q\): \(\{p/q \; | \; p, q \in \mathbb Z, q\neq 0 \}\) rational numbers
  • \(\mathbb R\): real numbers
  • \(\mathbb C\): complex numbers

Equivalent representations of \(\mathbb C\)

\(z = (x,y)\) belongs to the complex plane with \(x,y\in \mathbb R\).

So \(z=x\left(1,0\right)+y\left(0,1\right)\), i. e. \[z = x+i\, y\]

where \(x\) and \(y\) are the real and imaginary parts, respectively.

Equivalent representations of \(\mathbb C\)

\(i\) is the complex number represented by \(\left(0,1\right)\).

We say \(\mathbb R \subset \mathbb C\) by identifying the complex number \(x+i\cdot 0\) with the real number \(x\).

Addition in \(\mathbb C\)

Set \[\left(x_1,y_1\right)+\left(x_2,y_2\right) = \left(x_1+x_2,y_1+y_2\right)\]

That is \[\left(x_1 + i\, y_1\right)+\left(x_2 + i \,y_2\right) = \left(x_1+x_2\right)+i\left(y_1+y_2\right)\]

Multiplication in \(\mathbb C\)

We can use \(\times\) or \(\cdot\) or juxtaposition.

\[{\small \left(x_1, y_1\right) \cdot \left(x_2, y_2\right) = \left(x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2, y_1 x_2 + x_1 y_2\right)}\]

That is \[{\small \left(x_1 + i\, y_1\right) \cdot \left(x_2 + i \,y_2\right) = \left(x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2\right)+i\left(y_1 x_2 + x_1 y_2\right)}\]
