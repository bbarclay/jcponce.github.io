Topology

Take \( \Omega \subseteq \C\).

\(*\;\) \(z\in \C\) is an interior point of \(\Omega\) if \(\exists \epsilon>0\) such that \(B_{\epsilon}(z_0)\subset \Omega\)

(note \(\implies B_{\epsilon}(z_0)\subset \Omega\; \forall \epsilon^{\prime}\), \(0<\epsilon^{\prime}< \epsilon\)).

\(*\;\) \(z\in \C\) is an exterior point of \(\Omega\) if \(\exists \epsilon>0\) such that \(B_{\epsilon}(z_0)\cap \Omega \neq \emptyset\).

\(*\;\) \(z\in \C\) is a boundary point of \(\Omega\), \(z\in\partial \Omega \), if \(\forall \epsilon>0\):
\[ B_{\epsilon}(z_0)\cap \Omega \neq \emptyset \text{ and } B_{\epsilon}(z_0)\cap \Omega^c \neq \emptyset. \]
