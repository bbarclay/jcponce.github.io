Predator-prey systems


12.3 Eat or be eaten

  • In addition to modelling individual organisms with multiple life stages, systems of DEs can also model interactions between multiple species.
  • For example, the classical predator/prey problem in ecology considers what happens to the populations of two species when one preys on the other.
  • In laboratory situations there is control over these interactions. In na- ture, inter-species interactions are highly complex. We will first investi- gate a controlled example, then model a real interaction. The controlled example is very simple, but is not completely unreasonable.


Case Study 29: It's just not cricket

  • Initially the experiment comprises 60 frogs and 400 crickets. Each day:
    • 15 crickets are introduced into the experiment (modelling immigra- tion and birth of crickets);
    • 25% of the frogs each eat a cricket (death of crickets);
    • 12 frogs are removed (modelling emigration and death of frogs); and
    • for each 25 crickets present, one new frog is introduced (modelling birth and immigration of frogs based on available food resources).

Case Study 30: Snowshoe hares and Canadian lynx

Now we can use Euler’s method to model the population sizes.

Program specifications: Develop a Python program that uses Euler’s method with a step size of 0.01 years to model the populations of lynx and hares.



Program 12.2: Lotka-Volterra model of hares and lynx

# Uses Euler's method and Lotka-Volterra equations to model 
# populations of lynx and hare from 1900 to 1920.
from pylab import *

# Initialise variables for Euler's method.
stepsize = 0.01
times = arange(0 , 20+stepsize , stepsize )
a=0.484
b=0.028
c=1
d=0.032
hares = zeros(int(size(times))) 
lynx= zeros(int(size(times))) 
hares[0] = 30.0
lynx[0] = 4.0

Program 12.2: Lotka-Volterra model of hares and lynx

# Step through Euler' s method
for i in range(0,size(times)-1):
    dh = a * hares[i] - b * hares[i] * lynx[i]
    dl = - c * lynx[i] + d * hares[i] * lynx[i] 
    hares[i+1] = hares[i] + stepsize * dh 
    lynx[i+1] = lynx[i] + stepsize * dl

# Output graphs 
plot(times, hares, 'b-', linewidth=2, label='Hares') 
plot(times , lynx, 'r-', linewidth=2, label='Lynx') 
xlabel ("Time ( years since 1900)")
ylabel("Number of individuals (thousands)")
xlim (0 ,20)
ylim (0 ,70)
grid(True)
legend()
show()
