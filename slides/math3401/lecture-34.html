Residues at Poles

Theorem: If \(f\) has a pole of order \(n\) at \(z_0\), then we can write \[ f(z) = \frac{\phi (z) }{(z-z_0)^n} \] with \(\phi\) analytic and \(\phi(z_0) \neq 0\). Moreover \[ \underset{z=z_0}{\res} f =\frac{\phi^{(n-1)}(z_0)}{(n-1)!}. \]

Residues at Poles

Consider the function \(f(z)=\dfrac{1}{1-1/z}\), \(\quad z\neq 0\)

\(=\dfrac{z}{z-1}\), \(\quad z\neq 0 \;\qquad\)

\(=\dfrac{-z}{1-z}\qquad \;\;\quad\qquad\)

\(\;\;\qquad \qquad=-z\left( 1+z+z^2+ \cdots \right)\;\; 0\lt |z|\lt 1\)

\(= - z -z^2 - z^3-\cdots \;\quad\)

Residues at Poles

Taking  \(f(0)=0\) extends \(f\) to an analytic functions on \[\{z: |z|\lt 1\}.\]

So we have a removable singularity at \(0\).

Note: From previous Theorem, if \(m=1\) (simple pole)

\(\quad\qquad\qquad \underset{z=z_0}{\res}f(z) = \dfrac{\phi^{(0)}(z_0)}{0!}\) \(= \phi(z_0)\). \(\quad(**)\)
