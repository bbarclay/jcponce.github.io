The Derivative

Definition: Let $I$ be an interval, let $f \colon I \to \R$ be a function, and let $c \in I$. If the limit \begin{equation*} L := \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)-f(c)}{x-c} \end{equation*} exists, then we say $f$ is differentiable at $c$, that $L$ is the derivative of $f$ at $c$, and write $f'(c) := L$.

If $f$ is differentiable at all $c \in I$, then we simply say that $f$ is differentiable, and then we obtain a function $f' \colon I \to \R$.

The expression $\displaystyle\frac{f(x)-f(c)}{x-c}$ is called the difference quotient.
