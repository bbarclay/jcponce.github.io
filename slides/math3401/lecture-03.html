Complex conjugate

\(\conj{z} : \C \rightarrow \R\), \(x+i\,y \mapsto x-i\,y\).

If \(z=x+i\,y \), then \(\overline{z} = x-i\,y\).

Complex conjugate

Properties

(i) \(z = \conj{z} \leftrightarrow \Im(z)=0\), i. e. \(z\in \mathbb R\).

(ii) \(\conj{\left( \conj{z} \right)} = z \).

(iii) \(\conj{\left( z \cdot w \right)} = \conj{z} \cdot \conj{w} \).

(iv) \(\conj{\left( \frac{1}{z} \right)} = \frac{1}{\conj{z}}, \; z\neq 0 \).

Complex conjugate

Properties

(v) \( |z^2| = z \cdot \conj{z}, \; z\neq 0 \).

(vi) \( \Re(z) = \frac{z+\conj{z}}{2}\), \(\Im(z) = \frac{z-\conj{z}}{2i}\)

(vii) \(\conj{z + w} = \conj{z} +\conj{w} \).

Triangle inequality

\(|z+w|\leq |z| + |w|\)

Proof:

\(|z+w|^2=|z|^2+|w|^2-2|z||w|\cos A\)

\(|z+w|^2\leq |z|^2+|w|^2-2|z||w|\),
since \(\cos A \geq -1\).

Then \(|z+w|^2 \leq \left(|z|^2+|w|^2\right)^2\).

Take \(\sqrt{}\) in both sides and we are done \(\blacksquare\).

Polar coordinates

\( \left\{ \begin{array}{r} x = r\cos \theta \\ y = r\cos \theta \end{array} \right. \)

\[ \begin{align*} z &= r e^{i \theta} \\ &= r \left(\cos \theta + i \, \sin \theta \right)\\ &= r \text{cis}\theta \end{align*} \]

Argument

\(r e^{i \theta}\) formally follows from Taylor series of \(e^{i\theta}\).

Here \(\theta\) is an argument of \(z\) and we write \(\theta = \arg(z)\).

The \(\arg \) is not a (single-valued) function.

If \(\theta\) is an argument of \(z\), \(\Arg(z)\) is defined to be the unique value of \(\theta\) with \[-\pi < \arg (z) \leq \pi\]

Argument

\(\Arg(1+i)=\frac{\pi}{4}\), but \[\arg(1+i) = \ldots\, -\frac{7\pi}{4}, \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{9\pi}{4}, \ldots.\]

\(\Arg(-6)=\pi\).

\(\Arg(0)\) is undefined.

\(\arg(0)=\R\).

Argument

So \(\Arg\) is a function: \[\C\setminus \{0\} \rightarrow \R \]

\(\C\setminus \{0\} = \C^* = \C_*\).

Other functions

  • Modulus: \(|z|=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\)
    \[\text{e. g. } |7+i\,3| = \sqrt{49+9} = \sqrt{58}\] The modulus is a one-to-one function from \(\C\) to \(\R\). Indeed \(|z|:\mathbb C \rightarrow \left[0,\infty\right) \).

Other functions

  • \(\Re(z) =\) real part of \(z\).
  • \(\Im(z) =\) imaginary part of \(z\).
  • \( \Re : \C \rightarrow \R \) and \( \Im : \C \rightarrow \R \)
  • Examples: \[ \Re(7+3i) = 7, \quad \Im(7+3i)= 3. \]
