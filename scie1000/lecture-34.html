Predator-prey systems


1 Ordinary Differential Equations

1.8.3 Underdamping

For $p\lt \omega$ the characteristic equation

$ \lambda^2+2p\lambda+\omega^2=0 $

has two complex conjugate roots

$ \lambda_1=-p+i\sqrt{\omega^2-p^2}\;\;\text{and}\;\; \lambda_2=-p-i\sqrt{\omega^2-p^2}. $

Two linearly independent solutions are thus

$e^{-p t}\cos(\omega_p t)\;\;\text{and}\;\; e^{-p t}\sin(\omega_p t),$

where

$ \omega_p=\sqrt{\omega^2-p^2}$ $=\omega\sqrt{1-(p/\omega)^2}.$ 




# Uses Euler's method and Lotka-Volterra equations to model 
# populations of lynx and hare from 1900 to 1920.
from pylab import *
# Initialise variables for Euler's method.
stepsize = 0.01
times = arange(0 , 20+stepsize , stepsize )
a=0.484
b=0.028
c=1
d=0.032
hares = zeros(int(size(times))) 
lynx= zeros(int(size(times))) 
hares[0] = 30.0
lynx[0] = 4.0




# Step through Euler' s method
for i in range(0,size(times)-1):
    dh = a * hares[i] - b * hares[i] * lynx[i]
    dl = - c * lynx[i] + d * hares[i] * lynx[i] 
    hares[i+1] = hares[i] + stepsize * dh 
    lynx[i+1] = lynx[i] + stepsize * dl
# Output graphs 
plot(times, hares, linewidth=2, label='Hares') 
plot(times , lynx , linewidth=2, label='Lynx') 
xlabel ("Time ( years since 1900)")
ylabel("Number of individuals (thousands)")
xlim (0 ,20)
ylim (0 ,70)
grid(True)
legend()
show()
