Inverse function theorem

Theorem: Let $U \subset \R^n$ be an open set and let $f \colon U \to \R^n$ be a continuously differentiable function. Suppose $p \in U$ and $f'(p)$ is invertible (that is, $J_f(p) \not=0$). Then there exist open sets $V, W \subset \R^n$ such that $p \in V \subset U$, $f(V) = W$ and $f|_V$ is one-to-one. Hence a function $g \colon W \to V$ exists such that $g(y) := (f|_V)^{-1}(y)$. Furthermore, $g$ is continuously differentiable and \begin{equation*} g'(y) = {\bigl(f'(x)\bigr)}^{-1}, \qquad \text{for all } x \in V, y = f(x). \end{equation*}
