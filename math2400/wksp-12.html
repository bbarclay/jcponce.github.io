Problem 1

Using only the definition of the derivative, show that the following $f:\R^2\ra \R^2$ are differentiable at the origin and find their derivative at that point.

(a) $f(x,y):=(x-y,x+y),$         (b) $f(x,y):=(x^2-y^2, 2xy).$








Problem 2

Define a function $f:\R^2\ra \R$ as \[ f(x,y):= \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \dfrac{x^2y}{x^2+y^2} & \text{ if } (x,y)\neq (0,0),\\ 0 & \text{ if } (x,y)= (0,0). \end{array} \right. \]

  1. Show that the partial derivatives $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}$ and $\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$ exist at all points.
  2. Show that for all $u\in \R^2$ with $||u||=1$, the directional derivative $D_u f $ exists at all points.
  3. Show that $f$ is continuous at the origin.
  4. Show that $f$ is not differentiable at the origin.
